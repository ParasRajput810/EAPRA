## "The EAPRA Authors"

Copyright and other collective references use **"The EAPRA Authors"** as the
project's collective identity meaning *everyone who has contributed to the
codebase*, not a company and not a claim of plurality. It is the standard
open-source convention for holding copyright collectively across all
contributors (see the [`LICENSE`](./LICENSE) file).

In short:

- **"The EAPRA Authors"** = *who holds the copyright* every contributor.
- **Maintainers** (above) = *who is accountable* for direction, review, and releases.

This lets the project read as a collective effort (accurate contributions are
welcome and credited) while this file records, without inflation, that
stewardship currently rests with one person.

## Responsibilities of a maintainer

- Review and merge contributions; uphold the ADRs and the build-vs-integrate
  boundary (see [ADR-0003](./docs/adr/0003-build-vs-integrate.md)).
- Triage issues and shepherd releases.
- Enforce the [Code of Conduct](./CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).
- Keep significant decisions recorded in [`docs/adr/`](./docs/adr).

## Becoming a maintainer

EAPRA welcomes contributors. The path to maintainership will be formalized in
`CONTRIBUTING.md` (forthcoming). In brief: sustained, high-quality contributions
and review participation lead to an invitation from the existing maintainer(s).

## Contact

For general matters, open a GitHub issue. For conduct reports, use the contact
method in [`CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md`](./CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).
